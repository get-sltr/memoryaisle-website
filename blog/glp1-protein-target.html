Hitting protein when you can barely eat.

Three strategies that work even on the toughest day of your GLP-1 cycle — built around how appetite actually moves.

5 min read · Body composition

If you're on a GLP-1 — Wegovy, Ozempic, Zepbound, Mounjaro — you already know the trade-off no one warned you about. The medication does its job. The pounds come off. But for the first 24 to 48 hours after each dose, food feels physically impossible. A single chicken breast can feel like a Thanksgiving plate.

That's a problem. Studies of GLP-1 weight loss show roughly 39% of what you lose is lean mass, not fat. The single biggest lever you have against that number is protein. Hit your target consistently and you protect muscle. Miss it for weeks on end and you lose body composition along with body weight.

The standard target for anyone in a deficit is 1.6 to 2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day. For a 70 kg (154 lb) person, that's roughly 110 to 155 grams. Most people on a GLP-1 are clocking 40 to 60. The gap is real. Here's how to close it.

I · Protein first. Always.

The single most reliable change is structural: start every meal with the protein. Eat it before the rice, before the salad, before the bread basket. Your appetite has a hard ceiling on a GLP-1, and you don't know where it is until you hit it. The food you eat first is the food you actually digest.

This sounds obvious. It isn't. Most plates are built around carbohydrates — pasta with sauce, rice with topping, bread with filling. Reverse the architecture. Cook six ounces of salmon and serve it with a side of something else. If you only finish the salmon, you still hit 35 grams.

II · Liquid protein on the hard days.

The day after your injection, chewing is the enemy. Solid food sits in your stomach. The thought of meat is faintly nauseating. This is when most of your protein loss happens — you skip the meal, you skip the day, the gap accumulates.

Build a backup that doesn't require chewing. Options that work, in rough order of protein density per calorie:

  • Whey or casein protein shake — 25 to 30 g per scoop. Mix with milk for an extra 8 g.
  • Greek yogurt, plain, 0% or 2% — ~17 g per cup. Add a drizzle of honey or berries if it helps it go down.
  • Cottage cheese — 24 g per cup. Sounds like a 1970s diet food. Quietly excellent.
  • Skyr or kefir — 12 to 18 g per cup. Easier on the stomach than yogurt for some people.
  • Bone broth with collagen — 10 to 15 g per cup. Good when even cold liquids feel rough.

Two of these in a hard day adds 50 grams of protein with almost no chewing required. That's the floor. On easier days you build back to solid food and exceed it.

III · Distribute. Don't concentrate.

The instinct on a GLP-1 is to skip meals when you're not hungry and load up when you finally are. This is exactly backwards for muscle preservation.

Muscle protein synthesis — the process that actually builds and preserves muscle — runs in pulses, not pools. A 100-gram protein bomb at dinner doesn't bank protein for tomorrow. Your body uses what it can in the moment and oxidizes the rest. Three or four smaller hits of 25 to 35 grams across the day outperform one massive meal you can barely finish.

On a GLP-1 this also matches your appetite curve better. You can usually manage a small meal. You can rarely manage a large one. Plan for the small.

The real principle.

None of this is exotic. There's no supplement that will fix it for you. The structural truth of eating on a GLP-1 is that your appetite is a smaller container than it used to be, and protein has to be the thing you put in first. Everything else — the carbs, the fats, the variety — is what fits in around it.

Most people's biggest mistake is not the medication. It's continuing to eat the way they ate before the medication, just less of it. That's how you lose 30 pounds and keep almost none of it as muscle. The fix isn't more discipline. It's a different shape of plate.
