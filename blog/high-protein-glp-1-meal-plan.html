High-Protein GLP-1 Meal Plan: A 7-Day Menu to Lose Fat, Not Muscle

By MemoryAisle · June 3, 2026 · 7 min read

The hardest part of eating well on a GLP-1 isn't willpower — it's decisions. When your appetite is low, "what do I even eat?" becomes exhausting. So here's the thinking already done for you: a free 7-day high-protein meal plan built for Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, and Zepbound, designed to hit 100g+ protein a day in small, gentle meals that protect your muscle while you lose fat.

How this plan works

  • Protein target: roughly 100–130 g per day, at 25–35 g per meal. Protein is what keeps weight loss coming from fat, not muscle.
  • Structure: three small meals plus one snack — easier on a slow-emptying stomach than three big plates.
  • Protein first: always eat the protein portion before the sides.
  • Gentle by design: low-fat, not greasy, nothing that typically triggers nausea.
  • Hydration: 2–3 L water across the day, sipped between meals.
Tip: portions are a starting point, not a rule. Eat the protein first and stop when you're satisfied — leftovers are fine, under-eating protein is not.

Low-appetite swaps for the hard days

Some days you won't finish a plate. Keep the protein, drop the effort:

  • Swap any breakfast → a protein smoothie or a yogurt cup
  • Swap any lunch → a mug of broth with a soft-boiled egg, or a shake
  • Swap any dinner → cottage cheese, or a small portion of the protein only

Frequently asked questions

How much protein is in this plan?

Each day lands around 100–130 g, spread across three small meals and a snack at roughly 25–35 g each. Scale portions to match the target your provider or dietitian recommends.

What if I can't finish the meals?

Eat the protein first, then stop when satisfied. On hard days, swap a solid meal for a shake or Greek yogurt so you protect muscle without forcing food. Low-appetite swaps are listed above.

Does this work for Ozempic, Wegovy, and Zepbound?

Yes — protein first, smaller frequent meals, gentle low-fat foods, and steady hydration apply across all GLP-1 and GIP/GLP-1 medications.

Can I still lose muscle while following a plan?

It's possible if protein stays too low or you skip resistance training. Hitting your protein target consistently plus light strength work two to three times a week are the best protections.
