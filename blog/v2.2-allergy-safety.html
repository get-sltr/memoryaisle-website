Version 2.2 · May 2026

Every meal screened. Every allergen blocked.

Log your allergies once and Mira will never suggest a meal containing them. Plus editable goals, separate medication tracking, and a full visual refresh.

5 min read · Product update

If you have a food allergy, a suggested meal containing that allergen is not a UX problem. It is a potential medical emergency.

We built MemoryAisle 2.2 with that fact at the center. This update introduces a code-level allergy safety system that screens every meal before you see it, along with editable onboarding answers, separate medication and allergy pages, and a visual refresh across three more screens.

I · Allergy screening, enforced by code.

The new Allergies page lets you log your allergens: dairy, egg, peanut, tree nut, shellfish, fish, wheat, soy, sesame, and gluten. Once logged, every meal Mira generates is screened against your allergens before it reaches your screen.

This is not a prompt instruction that a language model might miss. It is a code-level filter that runs after every meal is generated and before it is ever displayed. If a meal contains a logged allergen, it is removed from the candidate set. Mira never shows it to you.

We would rather show you fewer meals than show you an unsafe one.

If a meal's allergen data has not been analyzed (older meals generated before this update), the filter treats it as potentially unsafe and excludes it. Fail safe, not fail open.

Every meal that passes the filter carries a visible reassurance tag naming your allergens explicitly. "Dairy and shellfish allergy safe." Soft white text on a lilac capsule. Reassurance, not a warning.

Existing users who had nut or shellfish allergies logged as dietary restrictions are automatically migrated to the new allergen system on first launch. No re-entry required.

II · Edit your answers, anytime.

Previously, the only way to change an onboarding answer was to re-run the entire flow. That risked resetting unrelated state and forced you to tap through screens you did not need.

Now, every answer you gave during onboarding lives permanently in Settings. Name, age, weight, goal weight, training level, movement notes, daily protein target, calorie target, water, fiber, product mode. Each one is individually editable. Change one thing without touching anything else.

Onboarding still runs once for new users. After that, Settings is the live editing surface. One source of truth. Two views over the same model.

III · Medication and allergy, separated.

Medication and allergy used to share a single page. They are now two separate entries in the menu.

Allergies is always visible. It is relevant for every user regardless of medication status. Log allergens and food preferences from one dedicated surface.

Medication opens with a simple question: "Are you currently taking any medication?" Not a GLP-1-specific question. If you say no, the tab stays collapsed. If you say yes, you pick from a list of GLP-1 medications or type any other medication into a free-text field. Dose, provider, pharmacy, and refill details follow.

Mira reads your medication and adapts meals accordingly. But she never suggests taking a substance, never gives dosing guidance, and never comments on the medication itself. The user sets the input. Mira responds to the nutrition around it.

IV · Visual refresh.

Reflection, Safe Space, and the meal screens now use the same day/night editorial design system as the rest of the app. The warm gray-to-gold gradient, the serif typography, the monospaced caps labels, the fireflies at night. Every screen feels like it belongs to the same product.

Safe Space keeps its privacy guarantee: Face ID only, on-device storage, excluded from backup, wiped from memory on background. The visual treatment changed. The security model did not.

V · The fix that matters.

The Save button on the weekly weigh-in screen was hidden behind the keyboard. A core feature, completely broken. This is fixed. The screen now adjusts for the keyboard properly, the Save button stays reachable, and a Done button above the number pad lets you dismiss it.
