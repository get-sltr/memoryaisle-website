What to Eat on GLP-1: The Complete Food Guide for Ozempic, Wegovy & Zepbound

By MemoryAisle · June 3, 2026 · 9 min read

A GLP-1 medication does one thing brilliantly: it shrinks your appetite. What it doesn't do is tell you what those fewer calories should be made of. Get that part right and you lose fat while keeping your muscle, energy, and nutrition intact. Get it wrong and you can lose weight on paper while quietly losing the muscle you'll want to keep. This is the complete, practical guide to what to eat on GLP-1.

Whether you're on Ozempic, Wegovy, Zepbound, or Mounjaro, the principles are the same. You'll eat less, so every bite has to work harder. The framework below makes that simple.

The GLP-1 plate, in one picture

Forget complicated rules. When you sit down to eat, build the plate in this order:

  1. Protein first — half the plate, and the first thing you eat.
  2. Non-starchy vegetables — the other half, for fiber and volume.
  3. A smart carb — a small portion of oats, whole grains, or fruit for energy.
  4. A little healthy fat — a few slices of avocado, a spoon of olive oil, a small handful of nuts.
Why protein first? Your appetite is your most limited resource on a GLP-1. Spend it on protein before you fill up — that single habit does more to protect muscle than anything else on this page.

How much protein you actually need

This is the number that matters most. A widely used dietitian target is roughly 1.2–1.6 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day, spread across meals at about 25–35 grams each. For many people that lands somewhere around 80–120+ grams a day. Hitting it is what keeps your body burning fat instead of breaking down muscle for fuel. Your exact number depends on your size and goals, so confirm it with your provider or dietitian.

Foods to prioritize vs. foods to limit

Prioritize

  • Lean protein: eggs, chicken, turkey, fish, lean beef
  • Dairy protein: Greek yogurt, skyr, cottage cheese
  • Plant protein: tofu, tempeh, lentils, beans
  • Non-starchy veg: broccoli, leafy greens, carrots, zucchini, tomatoes
  • Lower-sugar fruit: berries
  • Smart carbs: oats, quinoa, whole grains
  • Healthy fats: avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds

Limit

  • Sugary foods & drinks: soda, candy, big desserts
  • Refined carbs: white bread, pastries
  • Fried & high-fat foods: fries, fried chicken
  • Very rich, creamy, greasy dishes
  • Large portions of any food
  • Alcohol
  • Starchy items in excess: potatoes, corn, very ripe banana, mango

The "limit" list isn't about morality — it's about chemistry. GLP-1s slow how fast your stomach empties, so fried, fatty, and sugary foods sit heavily and tend to trigger nausea, while blunting some of the medication's blood-sugar benefit.

Fiber and hydration: the supporting cast

Fiber helps digestion and blood sugar, and aiming toward 25–38 grams a day is a good goal — but ramp up gradually. Too much fiber too fast can worsen GI side effects, so start with gentle sources like oats, bananas, zucchini, and cooked vegetables before moving to raw veg and beans. Crucially, increase your water at the same time: fiber without enough fluid makes constipation worse. Target roughly 2–3 liters of water daily, sipped between meals rather than with them.

A simple day of eating

  • Breakfast: Greek yogurt with berries and a sprinkle of granola (≈25 g protein)
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken over leafy greens, a little quinoa, olive oil (≈35 g)
  • Snack: Cottage cheese or a protein shake (≈20 g)
  • Dinner: Baked salmon, roasted broccoli and carrots, small portion of rice (≈35 g)

That's a satisfying, muscle-protecting day that most people can finish even with a reduced appetite — and it's well over 100 grams of protein.

The most common GLP-1 eating mistakes

  • Eating far too little overall. A quiet appetite isn't permission to skip meals — under-eating is how muscle disappears.
  • Carbs or sides first. You fill up before the protein goes in.
  • Forgetting to hydrate. Dehydration mimics and worsens fatigue and nausea.
  • No resistance exercise. Food protects muscle; lifting protects it more.
  • Living on ultra-processed "protein" snacks. Convenient, but whole foods bring the nutrients too.

Frequently asked questions

What foods should I prioritize on a GLP-1?

Build meals around lean protein, then add fiber-rich non-starchy vegetables and berries, smart carbs like oats and whole grains, and healthy fats such as avocado and nuts. Whole, minimally processed, protein- and fiber-rich foods are the foundation.

What foods should I limit on Ozempic or Wegovy?

Limit sugary foods and drinks, refined carbs, and fried or high-fat foods — they spike blood sugar, blunt the medication's benefit, and worsen nausea. Alcohol and very large portions are common triggers too.

How much protein should I eat?

A common target is about 1.2–1.6 g of protein per kg of body weight daily, spread at roughly 25–35 g per meal. Adequate protein is what protects lean muscle while you lose fat. Confirm your number with your provider or dietitian.

Do I need to count calories on a GLP-1?

Usually not obsessively — the medication already reduces intake. The bigger risks are under-eating protein and missing nutrients, so focus on protein and nutrient-dense whole foods rather than restricting further.
